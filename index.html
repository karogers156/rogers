Map 1: Identifying Impacted School Districts

For this exercise, I was asked to create a map highlighting Philadelphia School Districts that had over 1,200 students enrolled and were also within 0.1 miles of a bike network. The Philadelphia School District was interested in implementing a new initiative that encourages students to bike to school, but this would not be feasible for all students. To solve this issue, we identified which school districts in Philadelphia are close to bike networks. Additionally, we only selected districts with a large number of students, as the initiative would be most impactful on districts with a large student body. This map utilizes the "Selection by Attribute" and "Selection by Location" tools, which allow us to highlight only those districts that fit our criteria.

Map 3: Identifying New Locations for Healthy Food Stores

A prevalent issue in Philadelphia is food insecurity, especially for lower-income populations in urban areas. The City wants to find a potential new location for a healthy food store to alleviate this issue. Therefore, we want to find areas that are: included in the Philadelphia Empowerment Zones which receive targeted investments, within walking distance of a subway or regional rail station, and are not close to “competitors” or other stores that could limit the need for an additional store in the area. We can prove our case more easily if we identify an area within an empowerment zone, as the City would be providing program-qualified investments if they supported the development of a new store. We are more likely to receive approval and funding for a new location if it is within walking distance to public transportation, since many lower-income Philadelphians depend on public transport to access healthy grocery stores. Additionally, we are less likely to receive approval/funding if there are already healthy food stores in the area that are receiving governmental investments, as this may be seen as a waste of money and resources due to lower demand. To find these areas, we use different GIS tools (buffer, union, intersect) that highlight areas within the empowerment zones that are within walking distance to a public transit station and are not near competing stores.
